Gluten & Celiac Disease: Key Scientific Facts

🌍 Global Prevalence

Celiac disease affects approximately 1 in 100 people worldwide, though many cases remain undiagnosed due to mild or atypical symptoms.

🇺🇸 U.S. Statistics

Nearly 3 million Americans are estimated to have celiac disease, but up to 80% may be undiagnosed.

🧬 Genetic Link

The HLA-DQ2 and HLA-DQ8 genes are present in nearly all individuals with celiac disease, but having the gene does not guarantee developing the condition.

⚠ Autoimmune Reaction

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder. Gluten ingestion triggers immune cells to attack the small intestine’s villi, impairing nutrient absorption.

🥖 Gluten Composition

Gluten is made of two main proteins: gliadin and glutenin. Gliadin is primarily responsible for triggering immune reactions in celiac disease.

🍽 Treatment

The only current treatment is a lifelong strict gluten-free diet. Even small traces of gluten can trigger intestinal damage.

🔬 Enzyme Research

Proteolytic enzymes such as bromelain (from pineapple) and papain (from papaya) are being studied for their potential ability to break down gluten proteins.

🧠 Symptoms Beyond Digestion

Symptoms may include fatigue, anemia, headaches, joint pain, and neurological effects — not just digestive discomfort.
