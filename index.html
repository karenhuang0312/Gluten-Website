About Gluten Intolerance

What is Gluten? Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. It gives bread its chewy texture and can be hard to digest for some people.

Celiac Disease vs. Gluten Sensitivity: Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder that damages the intestine when gluten is eaten, while non-celiac gluten sensitivity causes symptoms like bloating and fatigue without intestinal damage.

Common symptoms: bloating, gas, cramping, nausea, and fatigue.

Disclaimer: This site does not replace medical advice. A gluten-free diet is essential for those with celiac disease.

Methodology: How I Made and Tested the Gluten Samples

Step 1: Dough Formation

  • Materials Needed: 100 g of all-purpose flour, 60 mL of distilled water
  • Measure 100 g of flour using a digital scale.
  • Add 60 mL of distilled water in a graduated cylinder.
  • In a bowl, combine the flour and water.
  • Mix and knead with a glass rod or your hands (wear gloves) until a uniform, soft dough forms.
  • Cover and let the dough rest for 15–30 minutes to allow the gluten network to develop.

Step 2: Starch Washing

  • Materials Needed: 300 mL of distilled water (×4)
  • Place the dough into a large bowl and add 300 mL of distilled water.
  • Gently knead the dough in the water to release the starch.
  • After 1–2 minutes, pour off the milky starch water into a separate container.
  • Replace with fresh water and repeat washing 4–6 times until the water becomes mostly clear.
  • Once done, you’ll be left with a rubbery, sticky mass. This is pure gluten.

Step 3: Collecting and Drying the Gluten

  • Gently squeeze the gluten mass to remove extra water.
  • Optional: Spread the gluten on a clean tray or Petri dish.
  • Allow it to air dry for 24–48 hours, or dry in an oven at under 50°C for faster drying.

Step 4: Treating with Natural Extracts

  • Cut the dried gluten into ~1 g pieces.
  • Place one piece in each Petri dish and add 2 mL of extract or combination mixture.
  • Leave for several hours at room temperature to allow enzyme reaction.

Step 5: Testing Gluten Breakdown

  • Use protein test strips (normally used for gluten detection) to measure gluten levels in the mixture.
  • Compare strip color to reference chart:
    • Green/Yellow = Low Gluten (0.3 g/L)
    • Moderate Green = 1–3 g/L
    • Dark = High Gluten (≥10 g/L)

This procedure helped determine which natural extracts, especially pineapple, papaya, and aloe were most effective in breaking down gluten proteins.
